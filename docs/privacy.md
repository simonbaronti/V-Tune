# V-Tune Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 28 May 2026_

## The short version

V-Tune doesn't collect any of your data. It doesn't have an account
system, it doesn't have analytics, it doesn't have ads, and it doesn't
phone home. Everything the app does happens on your device.

## The longer version

### What V-Tune uses

**Microphone access.** V-Tune asks for permission to use your
microphone so it can listen to the instrument you're tuning. The audio
is processed in real time on your device to detect frequencies and
drive the strobe display. **None of this audio is recorded, stored, or
transmitted anywhere.** It exists in your device's RAM for as long as
it takes to compute the next strobe frame — typically a few
milliseconds — and is then discarded.

**Local settings storage.** Your preferences — selected scale,
reference frequency, theme, settings panel state, etc. — are saved in
your device's local storage so they persist between sessions. This
data never leaves your device.

### What V-Tune does NOT use

- ❌ **No analytics.** We don't track which features you use, how long
  you use the app, or whether you open it at all.
- ❌ **No accounts.** There is no sign-up, no login, no email
  collection.
- ❌ **No ads.** No third-party advertising SDKs, no tracking pixels.
- ❌ **No network requests.** Apart from downloading the app itself,
  V-Tune does not connect to any server. The app works fully offline.
- ❌ **No third-party services.** No Firebase, no Google Analytics, no
  crash reporters, no anything.

### Children's privacy

V-Tune does not collect any personal information from anyone of any
age, so there is no special handling for users under 13. The app is
suitable for any user who can operate a touch screen.

### Changes to this policy

If we ever change what V-Tune does with data (we don't plan to, but if
this app ever gains a feature that requires it — say, syncing presets
across devices), we'll update this policy and bump the date at the top.
For material changes, a notice will appear in the app itself.
